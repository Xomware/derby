Overall take on notable horses:
	•	Fierceness if he gets a clean trip would win, but coming from the outside he’ll have to go through a decent amount of traffic and expend too much energy at the start. Very well could win, but nowhere near worth the odds you’ll have to take with him
	•	If we get an early fast pace, Sierra Leone is the best horse to mount a comeback around the final stretch. He’s navigated traffic in every prep race and ran his own race hitting the gas on his own terms. No reason he cant do that here
	•	Catching Freedom is also a must play to hit the board and has a shot to win. He’s improved every race and has plenty of distance in him to make it the full 1 1/8th
	•	Honor Marie had his best race barely losing to Catching Freedom in the prior race, running pretty much the same style. He also has a perfect post position, so he if absolutely alive to hit the board and/or win
	•	Just a Touch is lightly raced just like his triple crown winning dad Justify before he won the Derby. He’ll try and get out to the front of the pack and stalk a moderate pace in which he could take over at the end. If he can hold up the final stretch he has a shot to hit the board
	•	Do yourself and not let the buzz around the UAE horse Forever Young, it will be tempting, but even if he has a chance (which I don’t respect these foreign horses until one wins if ever), his odds will be beaten down by foreign bettors, so there will be no value
	•	Endlessly is a strong horse though has only run on grass or synthetic dirt (a couple recent winners have come off the synthetic dirt prep, so its not impossible). The trainer was adamant he should run in the turf race this weekend instead, which tells you all you need to know. However that is also factored into his odds, and adding him to the bottom of exotics could payout decent
