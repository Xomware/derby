Race Expectation

There are a lot of horses that are not only speed horses, but ones that have to have the lead in order to run their best race - some with a small chance to win (Rodriguez, Citizen Bull, East Avenue, American Promise) and some with zero chance (Coal Battle, Neoequos, Owen Almighty). If they do push the pace early as expected, it should open up the door for the closers to make a run at the end.

This is the first Derby I truly don’t see any horses that look unbeatable with a lot of the good closers looking similar in ability. Because of this I think whichever of the closing horses is able to sit down closest off the front group will have the advantage to break first and hold on with the last push. The two horses (also the top two in odds) that historically sit in that point in the field are Journalism and Sovereignty. The other closers have history of falling too far behind – which in the case of a true blazing opening speed meltdown, that would bring them well into play. Of the further out closers I like Final Gambit and Publisher – both of which would pay well.

Betting wise I don’t enjoy betting a favorite straight up – and don’t think Journalism nor Sovereignty will go off at a price I’d be willing to pay. I would take Journalism up to 4-1 and Sovereignty up to 8-1, but will be closer to 5-2 and 6-1 respectively. I think the value would be in taking a Publisher to hit the board (Show) and finish top 3. I would expect that to pay close to 5-1. Sovereignty to Show would pay about 5-2. At least one of these will cash. Then for fun I will sprinkle a longshot in Final Gambit to win at about 20-1. Since I do expect Journalism to win, I will only bet him on top in exacts and trifectas rather than outright to try and find some value.

Major Qualifying Race Recaps

Louisiana Derby (1 3/16 Mile)
	•	Result: Tiztastic, Chunk of Gold
	•	Tiztastic settled down far in the back after the break while Chunk of Gold pressed the lead the entire time. Tiztastic slowly made his move up the field after the first turn then took the lead during the final stretch and won by two lengths
	•	Chunk of Gold made a similar push for the lead at the last turn after having a good trip but couldn’t keep up with Tiztastic
	•	This derby being the longest prep race in length usually helps prove the winning horse can go the distance at Churchill

Arkansas Derby (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Result: Sandman, Publisher, Coal Battle
	•	This race set up extremely well for the closers Sandman and Publisher as it started with a blazing two horse sprint at the front which ultimately fell flat. Sandman was always two lengths ahead of Publisher who followed him the entire time stride for stride passing the field.
	•	This was an impressive field of horses and a legit win
	•	Coal Battle stalked the pace well and made a move in the last turn that fell short with the added distance.
	•	Sandman ran a little green in the last straightaway which tells me he was still a little immature with room to progress – but also gave up some straightaway speed
	•	Publisher had to make a very wide turn to find open space around the last turn and also had Sandman drift into his path a couple times which slowed some momentum. If he can somehow find a good trip he could be a contender to finish underneath. 

Wood Memorial (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Result: Rodriguez, Grande
	•	Rodriguez won wire-to-wire with a perfect trip which is hard to come by. He was able to separate himself from most of the field at the end so he still looked every bit as the best horse here
	•	Grande started mid pack and had to claw around the far edge to get into a spot to sprint on the last stretch. Was keeping pace with Rodriguez at the end, so take away his longer route and tougher trip, he wasn’t materially worse than Rodriguez.

Blue Grass (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Result: Burnham Square, East Avenue, 3rd, 4th, Render Judgment, Owen Almighty
	•	This was an incredibly tight race from front to back, maybe 6 lengths in total between first and last
	•	Burnham Square came back from starting and sitting in last place before slowly making his move to catch up to the pack and only caught East Avenue by a nose at the very end
	•	East Avenue and Owen Almighty sprinted out together and honestly held their pace pretty well. East Avenue was just a couple lengths holding his pace better. He barely got surpassed at the very end by Burnham Square
	•	Render Judgment did nothing impressive at all. Sat in the middle of the pack the full race and didn’t threaten to take over, although was never far off

Jeff Ruby (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Result: Final Gambit, Flying Mohawk
	•	Final Gambit had an awful break and was forced into last out of the gate. He navigating (probably the largest field of all the prep races) 12 horses to get himself a shot rolling out wide on the last turn, and still crushed the field while extending into the finish.
	•	This may have been a weak field, but Final Gambit looked great
	•	Flying Mohawk was irrelevant
	•	This is a synthetic dirt track which often causes a lot of bettors to discount this race as a legit indicator of how a horse will perform on dirt at Churchill Downs. While that does add an element of uncertainty, I was at the Rich Strike Derby where I also discounted him from coming out of turfway and missed out on a 100-1 winner. Clearly the Kentucky Derby trail cares about the Jeff Ruby or else it wouldn’t be given so many prep points and the trainers know how to get their horses ready just fine. Not saying its impressive winning here by any means, just don’t think we should discount a horse coming out of this race.

Santa Anita (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Result: Journalism, Baeza, Citizen Bull
	•	This race only had 5 horses.. not great practice to run down a Kentucky Derby field of 20 in my opinion. Journalism sat in 3rd the entire race before deciding to take over at the end and extending past the everyone except Baeza
	•	Baeza ran every bit of an impressive race as Journalism. He came out of the break poorly and ran pretty green at the start way out wide for no reason. Then something clicked for him and he looked like the strongest horse until Journalism out-ran him at the end by half a length.

Florida Derby (1 1/8 Mile)
	•	Results: Tappan Street (scratched from Derby), Sovereignty, Neoequos
	•	Neoequos jumped out to the early lead which will also be expected at the Derby due to his post position. He honestly kept his pace up pretty well and didn’t give in too much to Tappan Street or Sovereignty who ran him down
	•	Tappan Street positioned himself best and broke first which gave him the edge
	•	Sovereignty had to deal with a lot of early traffic before settling in to make a run at the end – which is great practice in a big field before the Derby
